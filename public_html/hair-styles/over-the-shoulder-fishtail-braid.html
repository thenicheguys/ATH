Over-the-Shoulder Fishtail Braid



Known as one of the most difficult braids, the Fishtail braid give us all hair envy, but most of us are also simply too scared to try it. Which is a shame because it’s so absolutely beautiful. So we’ve decided to ring together a few easy steps for you to follow to create your own Fishtail braid. It looks so sophisticated but it really is very easy, once you get the hang of it. Remember, practice makes perfect!

Step 1: Prep your hair
Braiding can be tough on your hair, with all the tugging that goes into it. So you’ll want to keep your hair nourished, and we recommend you use the Dove Pure Care Dry Oil Shampoo and Conditioner to keep it soft and healthy, with a radiant shine. To add texture to your hair, we suggest you use the TRESemmé Instant Refresh Translucent Dry Shampoo. This hairstyle works great when your hair has a bit of texture in it.

Step 2: Begin platting
Bring hair over onto one shoulder and tie with an elastic band (optional). Divide hair into 2 sections. Take a small piece from one section (furthest from the other section) and bring it over to the other section. Take a piece from thee second section (furthest from the first section) and bring it over to the first section. Remember that the size of the piece you take affects the size of the braid: small pieces equal smaller braid and big pieces equal a thicker braid. Ensure that you hold the hair steady between steps and keep it neat and tight.

Step 3: Repeat
Repeat these steps until you reach the ned of your hair and then you can tie it off with an elastic band.

Step 4: Finishing touches
If your braid is uneven you can balance out the bumps by tugging slightly on the smaller parts. To lock the braid in place (if you feel like it may come loose) you can use a bit of TRESemmé Salon Finish Firm Hold Hair Spray. Spray 20-30cm away from your hair.
